Dear Dusty,

You truly have changed my life so much. From being classmates in economics class during Y10, to being close friends throughout Y11 and Y12, and lastly evolving to the lovey-dovey couple in Y13, our friendship-turned-relationship means everything to me.

It’s sad that our relationship has to end this way. It's not like any of us wronged the other person, in fact, it has been going perfectly smooth and extremely strong. Undoubtedly, this has been a lingering thought that frequently intrudes my mind every time I try to fall asleep. It seems so unfair that life is pulling us apart, almost like it doesn't want us to stay together, and it makes me extremely upset.

However, what scares me the most is habit. On a normal day, we would be texting throughout the whole day - a never-ending conversation. Once these 4 days we share in Malaysia inevitably come to an end, it won't be the same anymore. I'm sure that we will keep on talking on Snap, but just the thought that we are no longer "boyfriend and girlfriend", and just "friends", will truly bother me. I'll have no one to send my "Goodnights", "Goodmornings", and "I love yous" to anymore. Withdrawal will only make me more conscious about the relationship that I have lost.

It looks like acceptance, no matter how hard it is, is going to be the only way forward. Of course, a huge part of me doesn't want to accept it, ignore and push away the thoughts of the end of our relationship, but what really keeps me grounded is remembering that we had a great run.

Unlike most couples who keep on pushing forward and eventually crashing and burning, I am glad that we have the foresight to realise that long distance won't work for us. This is truly the best way we could end things: on good terms.

The idea of this as a gift sparked in my mind when thinking about how I want to be remembered. Hopefully, if all this gift goes how I planned it, the Lego piece should resemble me close enough and the NFC card I placed on the back should make it scannable by your phone so that you can always access this gift easily.

I wanted to build a website because I wanted to make something that you can always keep close to you, a small Lego toy that carries all of our memories together.

I hope that you keep on coming back to this website whenever you want to relive the 4 years (but especially the last year) we had together as friends and as something more.

Additionally, I added a Spotify playlist (I think this is the modern equivalent to making a mixtape for someone) that has a bunch of curated lovey-dovey songs that make me think of us and our relationship.

As time goes on, I know that our relationship memories will start to slowly fade, so I want this gift to serve as a reminder and a throwback for you if you ever want to relive all the great memories we had.

To wrap this long but much-needed letter up, I want to say that I love you so much. Even if you are rereading this message after Malaysia, or in a few months, or even in a year, I want you to know that I love you and you will always have a special place in my heart.

Who knows? Maybe somewhere down the line we will add more to our timeline.

Love,
Antonio
